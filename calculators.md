# StrengthPeaking Calculator Index

This page lists the free strength training and powerlifting calculators available on StrengthPeaking.

The calculators are designed for lifters, coaches, and gym users who want quick training estimates without needing a spreadsheet or paid app.

## Training Max and Rep Calculators

### [1RM Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/1rm-calculator)

Estimate your one-rep max from the weight lifted and number of reps completed.

Useful for:

* Bench press
* Squat
* Deadlift
* Overhead press
* Other compound strength lifts

### [1RM Chart](https://strengthpeaking.com/1rm-chart)

View estimated 2RM to 10RM values from a known one-rep max.

Useful for comparing rep ranges, planning warm-ups, and choosing working weights.

### [Strength Metrics Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/strength-metrics)

Calculate a strength level from bodyweight, one-rep max, lift type, and sex.

Useful for comparing relative strength across bench press, squat, and deadlift.

## Strength Programming Calculators

### [Smolov Jr Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/smolov-jr-calculator)

Build a 3-week Smolov Jr cycle for bench press, squat, or another main lift.

Useful for short, high-frequency strength blocks.

### [5/3/1 Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/531-calculator)

Calculate Wendler 5/3/1 training weights from a training max.

Useful for planning percentage-based strength work.

### [DUP Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/dup-calculator)

Create daily undulating periodization training percentages across an 8-week block.

Useful for lifters who want varied intensity and volume across the training week.

### [Wave Loading Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/wave-calculator)

Build progressive wave loading sets from a target max.

Useful for strength blocks that use repeated waves of increasing intensity.

## Powerlifting Score Calculators

### [DOTS Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/dots-calculator)

Calculate a DOTS score for powerlifting performance comparisons.

Useful for comparing totals across different bodyweights.

### [Wilks Calculator](https://strengthpeaking.com/wilks-calculator)

Calculate a Wilks score based on bodyweight and total.

Useful for traditional relative strength comparisons in powerlifting.

## Notes

These calculators are intended as practical training tools. Results should be treated as estimates, not guarantees.

Training maxes, projected one-rep maxes, and powerlifting scores can vary depending on fatigue, technique, equipment, bodyweight changes, and training experience.
